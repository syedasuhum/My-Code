The Third Industrial Revolution, or Digital Revolution, began in the late 1900s and is characterized by the spread of automation and digitization through the use of electronics and computers, the invention of the Internet, and the discovery of nuclear energy.

The Third Industrial Revolution began in the '70s in the 20th century through partial automation using memory-programmable controls and computers.

The third revolution brought forth the rise of electronics, telecommunications and, of course, computers. The third industrial revolution opened the doors to space expeditions, research, and biotechnology through the new technologies.

Effects of Industrial Revolutions

  • Invention of Internet
  • Invention Of Digital TV
  • Mass-production
  • Invention of Web
  • Rise in Digital Technology

Cause of Industrial Revolutions

  • shift from mechanical and analogue electronic technology to digital electronics
  • Digital Computing
  • Communication technologies
